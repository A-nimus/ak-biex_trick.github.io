BINOMIAL EXPANSION TRICK BY
Akash Singh

STEP 1

: Observe the problem

It may be of one of the following forms:
  • (a+b)n + (a-b)n
  • (a+b)n - (a-b)n

STEP 2

: Notice the index n

It will be either odd or even.

Now find the total no. of terms in the given expansion using following rules:
  • Case 1:
    If the index n is odd then, the no. of terms(NOT) in:

    • (a+b)n + (a-b)n
    • (a+b)n - (a-b)n

    are given by,

    • $$NOT = {n + 1 \over 2}$$

  • Case 2:
    If the index n is even then, the no. of terms(NOT) in:

    • (a+b)n + (a-b)n

      • are given by,
      $$NOT = {{n \over 2 }+ 1}$$

    • (a+b)n - (a-b)n

      • are given by,
      $$NOT = {{n \over 2 }}$$
Once, you have successfully found the no. of terms the rest is as easy as खाला जी का घर ।

STEP 3

:

If the given problem is seperated by + sign then, just start expanding it evenly and multiply whole by 2 i.e. $$2\sum_{r=0}^n {^nC_r}a^{n-r}b^r$$
where,r is incremented by 2 i.e. r=r+2 until n

If the given problem is seperated by - sign then, just start expanding it oddly and multiply whole by 2 i.e. $$2\sum_{r=1}^n {^nC_r}a^{n-r}b^r$$
where,r is incremented by 2 i.e. r=r+2 until n
Example 1:
(a+b)6 + (a-b)6
=$$2({^6C_0a^6} + {^6C_2a^4b^2} + {^6C_4a^2b^4} + {^6C_6b^6})$$

Example 2:
(a+b)5 - (a-b)5
=$$2({^5C_1a^4} + {^5C_3a^2b^3} + {^5C_5b^5})$$
